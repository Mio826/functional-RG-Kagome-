## 2.2 Patching Benchmark

### Goal

Construct Fermi surface patches and extract eigenvectors.

### Tests Performed

* Patch positions lie on FS
* Patch ordering is continuous
* Eigenvectors extracted for each patch

### Problem Observed

Bloch eigenvectors exhibit:

* random U(1) phase
* discontinuous jumps between neighboring patches
* overlap phase ~0 or ~π

### Physical Meaning

This is expected:

[
|u_k\rangle \rightarrow e^{i\theta(k)} |u_k\rangle
]

But causes issues downstream.

### Conclusion

✔ Patching geometry correct
⚠ Gauge structure problematic

# 3. Core Problem: Bloch Gauge Inconsistency

---

## 3.1 Nature of the Problem

Eigenvectors from diagonalization are defined up to:

[
|u_k\rangle \rightarrow e^{i\theta(k)} |u_k\rangle
]

Across patches:

* θ(k) is random
* leads to discontinuous gauge

---

## 3.2 Observed Symptoms

* Patch overlap phase jumps ~π
* Vertex entries fluctuate
* Kernel entries fluctuate
* Form factors noisy

---

## 3.3 Important Insight

This is NOT a bug.

It is:

> A representation-level ambiguity (gauge freedom)

But:

> It must be controlled for numerical stability.

---

# 4. Solution: Parallel Transport Gauge Fix

---

## 4.1 Method

For ordered patches:

[
|u_{p+1}\rangle \rightarrow e^{-i \arg(\langle u_p | u_{p+1} \rangle)} |u_{p+1}\rangle
]

---

## 4.2 Effect

Ensures:

[
\langle u_p | u_{p+1} \rangle \in \mathbb{R}^+
]

---

## 4.3 Interpretation

This constructs a:

> Smooth gauge (continuous frame) along FS contour
